Flying Enemy

"The second enemy I developed for Hellcaster"

About

The Bat Enemy was created to introduce fast-moving aerial combat encounters that contrasted with grounded enemy types. I was responsible for the character's modelling, texturing, rigging, and animation, with a focus on creating clear silhouettes and fluid movement that remained readable in gameplay.



Textures

This model served as an artistic evolution from the previous skeleton model I produced. I approached the texturing for this model differently; instead of a photo-bashing technique, I went for a more hand-painted, ethereal look. This more closely reflected the concept art I was given.

  • Adapted the concept artist's use of colours and texturing into a texture sheet.
  • UV unwrapped a complex model to accommodate textures.
  • Hand-painted textures.

Modelling

As the concept art this time around was asking for a more complex silhouette, special attention was given to ensuring the silhouette of the model accurately represented the concept art. Consideration also had to be given to the geometry of the model, to ensure proper deformations whilst animating

  • Adapted the concept artist's initial sketch into a realised model.
  • Communicated with the concept artist and iterated upon feedback.
  • Optimised geometry to prepare the model for rigging and animation.
  • Created strong silhouette readability.


Gallery

Rigging

  • Built a custom rig using IK's to animate the model.
  • Created custom animation controllers that help simulate wing movement.
  • Had to iterate on the wings mesh to accommodate the rig.

Gallery

Animation

  • Communicated with the lead enemy designer to ensure all attack states were animated.
  • Animated the bat's idle, long-range attack and short-range attack animations.
  • Utilised Blender's 'Action Editor' and 'Dope Sheet' to streamline the importing of the animations into Unity.
