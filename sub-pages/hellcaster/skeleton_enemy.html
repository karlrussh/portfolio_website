Skeleton Enemy

"One of the main enemies in Hellcaster"

About

The skeleton enemy was designed to be a core enemy in the game Hellcaster, which was designed to reinforce the game's dark fantasy aesthetic. I was responsible for the full asset pipeline, including 3D modelling, UV unwrapping, texturing, rigging and in-engine implementation.



Textures

To help build and design the game's retro aesthetic, I utilised a photo-bashing workflow when creating the textures. This helped achieve a gritty, detailed appearance whilst maintaining the stylised look of the project. This way of working was undertook to emulate older retro games that utilised similar techniques.

  • Developed a photo-bashing workflow to help define the visual look of enemies in the game.
  • Creatively adapted the concept artist's sketches into a texture sheet.
  • Limited the colours and resolution of the textures to help produce a low-poly aesthetic.

Modelling

During the modelling process, particular attention was given to creating a readable silouhette that could be easily recognised by player's during combat.

  • Adapted the concept artist's initial sketch into a realised model.
  • Maintained a low-poly topology consistent with the game's art direction.
  • Created strong silhouette readability.
  • Optimised gemometry for real-time performance.
  • Ensured the mesh had ample topology to deform correctly during animation.
