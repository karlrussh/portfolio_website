Enemy models

"The two character models I developed for Hellcaster"

Overview

Initial talks between the art team and the project lead confirmed that the game was to attempt to reproduce a low-poly dark fantasy art style. Inspired by pieces of work such as Vermis and its dark-fantasy aesthetic, I began to develop the art style the characters of Hellcaster will adopt.

Concept art was developed by the 2D artists in the group and handed to me to adapt into 3D characters.

Skeleton concept art by Joe. Bat concept art by Olesya.


Skeleton Enemy

The first 3D character I set out to create was the skeleton enemy. This was intended to be a common enemy within the game, meaning its design should be readable and artistically consistent.

Software: Blender, Photoshop
Poly Count: 1664 tris
Workflow: Low-poly + Photo-bashing
Texture Size:

  • Body: 512x256
  • Accessories: 256x256
  • Guitar: 256x256

Screenshot of the skeleton model in engine.


Design Goals :

  • Create a model that accurately reflects the concept art and the intended low-poly style.
  • Translate the concept into a 3D silhouette that is easily readable by the player.
  • Adopt a texturing style that helps reinforce the game's dark fantasy aesthetic.
  • Support animation by ensuring enough geometry is present for proper deformation.

What I achieved:

  • Established a reusable workflow for modelling, texturing, and optimising low-poly enemy characters.
  • Adapted 2D concept art into a game-ready 3D asset while preserving silhouette readability.
  • Iterated on proportions and materials through feedback with the concept artist.
  • Optimised topology and edge flow to support rigging and animation deformation.

Work in progress images of the skeleton model.


Did I achieve my goals?

“Adopt a texturing style that helps reinforce the game's dark fantasy aesthetic.”

    Yes. I adopted a texturing style called 'photo-bashing', in which I blended photographs of skeletons, leather jackets, and electric guitars to create the textures for the final model. This technique of using real images was also used in older retro games, which helps achieve the retro, gritty look we sought.

    In the future, however, I'd like to incorporate more traditional painting techniques to achieve a more curated look, rather than relying solely on sourced photos.

“Support animation by ensuring enough geometry is present for proper deformation.”

    This goal was achieved through constant iteration and feedback between the team. Initial iterations of the model had more realistic proportions, making it too thin and hard to spot in the fast-paced gameplay of Hellcaster. To remedy this, time was spent readjusting the widths of certain elements, such as the arms and upper torso, to improve player readability.

Bat Enemy

The Bat Enemy was created to introduce fast-moving aerial combat encounters that contrasted with the game's grounded enemy types. As a flying enemy, its design needed to remain visually readable during fast-paced gameplay while maintaining consistency with the game's dark fantasy aesthetic.

Software: Blender, Photoshop
Poly Count: 1472 tris
Workflow: Low-poly + Painted
Texture Size:

  • Body: 512x256
  • Wings: 256x256

Screenshot of the bat enemy model.


Design Goals :

  • Create a model that accurately reflects the concept art and intended stylised aesthetic.
  • Develop a clear and readable silhouette that remains recognisable during aerial movement.
  • Create smooth and expressive animations to support fast-paced combat encounters.
  • Ensure the model's geometry supports proper rigging and deformation during animation.
  • Experiment with a more hand-painted texturing style that better reflects the concept art.

What I achieved:

  • Adapted the concept artist's initial sketch into a realised 3D model.
  • Communicated with the concept artist and iterated upon feedback throughout development.
  • Created hand-painted textures that reflected the ethereal tone of the concept art.
  • UV unwrapped a complex model to accommodate the texture sheet.
  • Optimised topology to support rigging and animation deformation.
  • Created a custom rig using IK controls to animate the wings and body.
  • Iterated on the wing geometry to better accommodate animation requirements.
  • Created custom controllers to help simulate wing movement.
  • Animated the bat's idle, long-range attack, and short-range attack animations.
  • Worked with the lead enemy designer to ensure all combat states were represented in animation.
  • Used Blender's Action Editor and Dope Sheet to streamline animation export into Unity.

Work in progress images & gifs of the bat model.


Did I achieve my goals?

“Experiment with a more hand-painted texturing style that better reflects the concept art.”

    Yes. Unlike the Skeleton Enemy, which used a photo-bashing workflow, this model utilised a more hand-painted texturing approach. This allowed the final asset to more closely resemble the original concept art while also helping establish a more ethereal visual style. The process also helped expand my texturing workflow by introducing more traditional painting techniques, rather than relying primarily on sourced photographic textures.

“Develop a clear and readable silhouette that remains recognisable during aerial movement.”

    This goal was achieved through close iteration between modelling and animation. The concept art featured a much more complex silhouette than previous enemy designs, meaning extra care had to be taken to preserve readability during movement. The wings, ears, and upper body proportions were continuously adjusted throughout development to ensure the enemy remained visually distinct during fast-paced gameplay.

“Ensure the model's geometry supports proper rigging and deformation during animation.”

    This was achieved through iterative topology adjustments during the rigging phase. Additional geometry was added around the wing joints to support smoother deformation during flapping animations, while less important areas were simplified to avoid unnecessary complexity. The final rig used IK controls and custom animation controllers to create more fluid and controllable wing motion.
