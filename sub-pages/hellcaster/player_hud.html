Player HUD

"My contribution to the visual feedback of Hellcaster"

About

As in any other first-person shooter game, the player needs a heads-up display to view their character's stats and actions. For this, I designed the initial concept sketched for the player HUD and designed and integrated the health bar and combo meter.

  • Designed concept sketches that helped the team implement the player HUD.
  • Developed a diegetic approach to displaying player stats.
  • Helped develop ideas such as applying a screen tint to show more clearly the current spell type and having the center crosshair act as the BPM counter.
  • Designed art assets and shaders, to help fit the UI element into the music theme of the game.
  • Worked closely with systems developers to ensure the UI element accurately interacted with their system.
  • Iterated upon feedback to help consolidate the UI element more into the game's style.


Health Bar

The health bar was designed to be as diegetic as possible. In the initial design sketches, you can see clear consideration of the game’s musical theming. The player’s health is signified by a sound wave that dynamically shifts colour, speed and height based on current health. This not only integrates the music theme in a fun way but also creates a UI element that clearly communicates the needed information at a glance.

Combo Meter

Like the health bar, the main goal going into designing the combo meter was to create a UI element that fit within the game's musical theme. As the main goal of the game was to stay on beat, it made thematic sense to display this notion with musical items, in this case, a vinyl disc. This is amplified by sound waves emitting from around the vinyl disc, which changes colour based on player performance. A text box appears whenever the player hits on beat, showing a performance metric indicating how on beat their attack was. Here, I had to work closely with another colleague's combo system, integrating my back-end UI code into their combo manager.
